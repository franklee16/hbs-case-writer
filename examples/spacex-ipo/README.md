# SpaceX IPO — Worked Example

This folder is the flagship worked example for the `hbs-case-writer` skill. It demonstrates the upgraded skill standards: **primary-document coverage** (three segments, not two), an **analysis framework** (segment build, lever table, triangulation, physical-unit translation), **quantitative discipline** (offer-price vs trading-price multiples distinguished, share-count basis stated), and **decision depth** (a buy-side analyst setting a price target, not a retail flip-or-hold).

## Contents

- **`SpaceX_IPO_Case.md`** — the case (v2, framework-driven). Protagonist: Priya Raman, a growth-equity analyst recommending a price target and position to her investment committee.
- **`SpaceX_IPO_Slides.tex` / `.pdf`** — the companion Beamer teaching deck. This deck is from the **v1 retail "flip-or-hold"** framing of the same IPO ("SpaceX: To the Moon, or Back to Earth?") and is kept as a slide template reference. It predates the v2 case and is not slide-for-slide aligned with `SpaceX_IPO_Case.md`; regenerate it from the v2 case if you need a matching deck.
- **`figures/`** — exhibit figures used by the slides.

## The teaching point the example is built around

The case's angle comes straight from the primary document: the marketing narrative is Mars, but the prospectus's disclosed economics are largely AI (AI-family terms outnumber Mars-family terms roughly 27:1; 93% of the claimed $28.5T TAM is AI; Mars is not named among uses of proceeds). The framework then shows that *no cash-flow lever* closes the gap to the offer price within its evidence range, only a discount-rate revision that the evidence cannot reject. The IPO debate is a disagreement about the price of risk, conducted where estimation cannot settle it.
